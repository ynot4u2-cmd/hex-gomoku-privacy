Hex Gomoku

Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how Hex Gomoku handles information when you use the app. Hex Gomoku is designed as a local strategy game and currently stores gameplay-related information primarily on your device.

1. Overview

Hex Gomoku is a mobile strategy game. At the current stage, the app does not require you to create an account and does not provide online multiplayer. Most gameplay data is stored locally on your device to support app features such as player profiles, preferences, and Elo progress.

2. Information Stored on Your Device

The app may store the following information locally on your device:

  • player profiles and player names
  • Elo ratings and Elo history
  • game settings and app preferences
  • board size, AI settings, and similar gameplay configuration
  • local game history or move log data used by app features
  • debug or test settings when using development versions of the app

3. Personal Data

Hex Gomoku is not intended to collect sensitive personal data. The app does not ask you to register an account, and the information stored in the app is generally limited to gameplay and preference data that you choose to create inside the app.

4. Data Sharing

At the current stage, Hex Gomoku does not sell your personal data. If the app offers system sharing features, such as sharing a game result or screenshot, the content is only shared when you actively choose to do so using your device's sharing options.

5. Third-Party Services

The app may in the future integrate services such as Google Play billing, rating links, analytics, crash reporting, or other platform features. If such services are added and they affect data handling, this Privacy Policy will be updated accordingly.

If you enable app store distribution, testing, purchases, analytics, ads, or cloud features later, make sure this section is updated before release.

6. Children

Hex Gomoku is a general-audience strategy game. If you intend to specifically target children, you should review the applicable app store rules and adapt this policy if additional disclosures are required.

7. Data Security

Reasonable efforts are made to keep the app stable and to avoid unnecessary data collection. However, no method of electronic storage or transmission is completely secure.

8. Your Choices

Because most app data is stored locally, you can generally remove that data by clearing the app's storage or uninstalling the app from your device, subject to your device and platform behavior.
